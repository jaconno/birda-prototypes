Caveats and limitations
  • None of these providers publish official rate cards. All estimates are based on third-party procurement benchmarks, Vendr anonymised transaction data, and community-reported contracts.
  • Actual quotes will vary significantly based on negotiation leverage, contract length, feature tier selected, and whether you are a new or returning customer.
  • Braze, Iterable, and MoEngage all require a sales conversation to get a real number. Expect 2–4 week sales cycles before a formal proposal.
  • The 4-month data window is short. MAU is growing (38k → 62k Jan–Apr 2026); providers will likely want to model projected annual peak MAU, not current figures. This changes the tier and the price.
  • Customer.io's "unlimited push + in-app" is subject to a fair-use policy of 60× profile count per month. At 62k profiles that cap is 3.72M — well above current combined push & in-app volumes.
  • Braze and MoEngage typically negotiate on multi-year commits (15–25% discount), which could meaningfully reduce the ranges shown.
  • GBP conversion uses a flat 0.80 rate. All contracts will be denominated in USD.
  • The 80k and 110k scenarios assume email, push, and in-app volumes scale linearly with MAU. In practice, engagement rates may improve or decline at scale — treat these as directional cost ceilings, not forecasts.
  • Pushwoosh scenario costs are formulaic and precise. All other providers' scenario costs are modelled estimates based on published tier ladders or community benchmark rates — they should be verified with a direct quote at each threshold.
  • Implementation, migration, professional services, and dedicated CSM costs are excluded and can add £20k–£80k one-time for enterprise platforms.
Methodology

Volume data was provided directly from an internal Google Sheet export (Jan–Apr 2026), covering emails sent, push sent, in-app sent, and MAU for the Explore product. Monthly averages were computed across the four months.

Provider pricing estimates were derived by searching pricing pages, Vendr procurement benchmarks, SaasBlueBook (last verified March 2026), and published third-party analyses in May 2026. Where published pricing exists (Customer.io, OneSignal, CleverTap Essentials tier), that was used directly. Where only ranges exist (Braze, Iterable, MoEngage), the ranges reflect community-reported contract values for comparable MAU brackets.

Peak MAU (62k) was used as the basis for tier assignment, as enterprise providers contract on peak-month MAU, not average. Annual cost estimates multiply monthly platform costs by 12, with no adjustments for growth, overages, or negotiation discounts.
