The house sparrow (Passer domesticus) is a bird of the sparrow family Passeridae, found in most parts of the world. Females and young birds are coloured pale brown and grey, and males have brighter black, white, and brown markings.
Identification tips
They have a varied diet, including fish, crustaceans, as well as some plants and are also scavengers, consuming carrion and food. Females and young birds are coloured pale brown and grey, and males have brighter black, white, and brown markings.
